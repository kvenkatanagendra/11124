Tea is a fragrant beverage made from the dried or fresh leaves of the evergreen shrub Camellia sinensis,
which is indigenous to China, India, and other East Asian nations.
The leaves of the Camellia taliensis are also infrequently used to make tea.
It is the most extensively drank beverage in the world after water.
